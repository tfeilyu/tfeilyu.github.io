Paper Information

  • Nuclear radiation (NR), which is energy emitted from atomic nuclei during decay, sNuclear radiation (NR), which is energy emitted from atomic nuclei during decay, has a significant impact on human health and the environment.

  • Precise forecasting of nuclear radiation is crucial for both individuals and governments. In this study, we introduce NRFormer, an innovative framework designed for forecasting nuclear radiation levels across Japan, addressing critical challenges such as the non-stationary nature of radiation patterns and the uneven distribution of monitoring stations. Incorporating a non-stationary temporal attention module, an event-aware spatial transformer, and a meteorological prompting module, NRFormer adeptly captures complex spatio-temporal dynamics in nuclear radiation. Tested on two comprehensive datasets, NR1D, our approach demonstrates marked superiority over ten state-of-the-art models. This research not only advances the predictive accuracy in environmental safety and public health but also provides a significant methodological contribution to the field of nuclear radiation forecasting, offering insights for enhanced emergency response and environmental monitoring strategies.
